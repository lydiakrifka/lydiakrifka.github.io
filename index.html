Lydia

Lydia Krifka-Dobes is a computational artist, sound designer, and researcher based in Barcelona. She works creatively as a systems & multimedia architect of multimodal computational systems. Her practice centers on creative coding in PlugData and Supercollider, where she builds custom tools for live performance and sound design. In 2025, she completed a Bachelor’s degree in Sonology at the Royal Conservatoire in The Hague. More recently she joined Sound and Music Computation at Music Technology Group at Universitat Pompeu Fabra (UPF) researching Vulkan, DSP, and concorrency where audio, graphics, and data are morphable as one substrate. She has performed internationally, including festivals such as Venice Biennale and Radialsystem Berlin. More recently, Lydia has become heavily involved as an organiser for youth hackathons Berlin and Barcelona's poetic computation scene, participating in the curation of such as Jugend Hackt at the intersection mathematics, music, and poetic hardware. Her approach combines design of musical instruments and environmental sensing interfaces, microbial research, attention to site and non-human ears, field recordings, sonification mapping, electroacoustic composition and other mediums. Adomas’s further sound practice extends towards electroacoustic performances, composition for theater as well as sound engineering for live acts. Her solo works and collaborations have been presented in various spaces across Lithuania, Italy, Belgium, Germany, The Netherlands, UK, Czechia, also international festivals such as "Common Sound 2023", Venice Architecture Biennial. Currently Lydia is a researcher at the Music Technology Group at UPF. She’s also involved with Berlin DIY communities.

Classes Sound and Music Computation

DSP for sound and music
Methods for analysing and transforming audio using spectral and time-domain techniques, directly relevant to spatial sound, ambisonics, and sound design workflows. upf program

ML for sound and music
Deep learning methods for audio and music representations, useful for feature extraction, corpus-based composition, and experimental models on multilingual speech and phonetics.

Generative algorithms for sound and music
generative ai approaches in symbolic and audio domains, aligned with my practice in algorithmic composition, stochastic systems, and interactive sound installations.

Music Information Retrieval
techniques for describing audio content (timbre, rhythm, harmony, mood, etc.) and building searchable sound corpora, close to my work with large language and audio datasets.

advanced computing techniques for sound and music
state-of-the-art methods for automatic processing of music content, bridging my low-level c++ / dsp interests with higher-level creative tools.

advanced interface design
paradigms and tools for multimodal interfaces between humans and computational systems, relevant for sensor-based installations, live-coding performance, and experimental instrument design.

music perception and cognition
empirical and computational perspectives on how humans perceive sound, rhythm, tonality, and emotion in music — directly connected to my interest in linguistic prosody and listening behaviour.
