Reflections on Spatial Audio

After spending the last few weeks experimenting with IEM plugins for spatial audio, I've been thinking about how we perceive sound in multichannel environments. The experience of working with the WaveField Synthesis system at Game of Life has completely changed my approach to composition.

There's something fascinating about the intersection of algorithmic composition and physical space. When you have 192 speakers at your disposal, the question isn't just "what should this sound like?" but "where should this sound exist?"

241115

Notes on Machine Listening

Been diving deep into the work at Music Technology Group lately. The intersection of machine learning and audio analysis continues to reveal surprising connections between how machines and humans perceive sound.

Working on a new project that uses PyTorch to analyze formant structures across different languages. The patterns that emerge are beautiful and unexpected.

On Improvisation and Autonomy

Thinking about the relationship between human and machine improvisation. When we talk about SuperCollider as a performance tool, we're really talking about a collaborative partner that has its own voice.

The question isn't about control, but about dialogue. How do we create systems that listen as much as they speak?
